Customer Problem

Pros who buy shared data leads from Angi, Thumbtack, and Google LSA are paying ~$1,000 per booked customer because leads are sent simultaneously to 3‚Äì7 competitors and the Pro is often in the field when the homeowner calls ‚Äî the window to win is gone before the Pro even knows they have a lead.

Exclusive inbound phone calls solve all three failure modes (exclusivity, availability, freshness), but the mechanism to buy them inside HCP doesn't exist. Pros who want call buying today go directly to ResultCalls or eLocal outside HCP ‚Äî we have no visibility, no attribution, and no retention hook on that spend.

‚ö†Ô∏è Two prerequisites must ship before any option can launch
  • Prerequisite 1 ‚Äî CSR AI spam billing fix: Purchased calls that route into CSR AI and get billed as spam destroy the ROI math instantly. Eng has LLM-based spam classification (~6% flagged); billing exemption is the missing piece.
  • Prerequisite 2 ‚Äî SMS follow-up for new prospects: Not every purchased call will book live. AI-driven SMS follow-up (Feature #6, 3-month agentic roadmap) must work for new prospects, not just saved HCP customers.

Technical Problem

What we know from research

  • eLocal integration already exists in HCP (owned by #eng_job_inbox / Christian Hong) ‚Äî scope is unknown: live call routing or data lead records only? This determines the build delta between all three options.
  • CSR AI is built on VAPI / Twilio. Purchased calls need Pro-specific DID numbers that VAPI can answer and assign to the correct CSR AI context.
  • LLM spam classification exists (~6% flagged) but billing exemption has never shipped.
  • Missed-call SMS fires for existing HCP customers only ‚Äî new prospect numbers get nothing.

What we need to validate with engineering

  • What does the current eLocal integration actually do ‚Äî live call routing or data lead records only?
  • Can VAPI/Twilio provision Pro-specific tracking numbers at scale for marketplace routing?
  • What is the billing infrastructure path for pass-through spend (Pro funds balance ‚Üí eLocal charges ‚Üí HCP invoices Pro)?
  • How does CSR AI context get populated for eLocal calls vs. organic? Does source tag change the agent flow?
  • What's the effort to extend CSR AI's intake to accept a source parameter that drives job attribution?
Critical unknown affecting all estimates

All three options assume the eLocal integration can be extended to live call routing. If the current integration is data-only, effort estimates increase materially ‚Äî particularly for Options 1 and 2.

Recommendation

PM Recommendation ‚Äî for use before the eng review meeting

Recommended path: Option 2 (Full Native Marketplace), with Option 1 as the prerequisite validation sprint.

Option 2 is the right product ‚Äî it gives Pros a native call-buying experience inside HCP, creates a margin opportunity, drives CSR AI attach rate, and positions HCP defensibly. Option 3 is architecturally correct long-term but is premature abstraction before call buying demand is proven at all. Option 1 alone is not a durable product ‚Äî it requires Pros to have an existing eLocal relationship and gives HCP no retention or margin.

The sequencing that makes sense

1
Ship Option 1 first as a 1-sprint POC ‚Äî validates the eLocal ‚Üí CSR AI routing path, gets attribution data flowing, and proves (or disproves) that call buying economics work before the larger Option 2 build.
2
If Option 1 POC shows strong conversion (>30% book rate on purchased calls, Pros rebuy), accelerate to Option 2.
3
If Option 2 reveals eLocal pricing as a competitive constraint, architect Option 3's routing layer as Phase 2 of Option 2 ‚Äî not a separate greenfield build.

Hard prerequisites before any option ships

CSR AI spam billing fix must be live SMS follow-up for new prospects live or close
