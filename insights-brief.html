01 Problem Definition

The dominant pain in HCP's VOC around marketing spend is not "I need more leads." It's "I buy leads and they don't turn into jobs." The distinction matters: Pros aren't shopping for lead volume — they're shopping for booked appointments. A purchased inbound phone call is structurally closer to a booked job than any data lead, because the homeowner is already on the phone, already high-intent, and already waiting for someone to answer.

The 3-Part Failure Loop

1
Competition before the race starts: Shared lead sent to 3–7 pros simultaneously — the homeowner's phone starts ringing before the Pro even knows the lead exists.
2
Pro is in the field: Can't call back immediately — on a job, driving, under a crawl space. Even a 10-minute delay drops conversion by 400%.
3
Homeowner already booked: The first pro who responded wins. 78% of homeowners go with whoever calls first.
→ Pro pays for a lead they never had a real shot at. Loop repeats.

This loop is not solvable with better CRM habits or follow-up reminders. The structural problem is that the lead was never exclusive and the window to win closed before the Pro knew they had a lead.

An exclusive inbound call solves all three failure modes simultaneously

Exclusive → homeowner is only talking to this Pro → no competition problem

Live call → homeowner is on the phone right now → no speed-to-lead race

AI answers every call → 100% answer rate → no missed-call revenue loss

The Proposed Solution

Build a call buying marketplace inside HCP — letting Pros purchase exclusive inbound calls from a third-party provider (eLocal as starting partner), routed directly to HCP's AI Virtual Agent for guaranteed 24/7 answer and booking. Two prerequisites must be in place first:

Prerequisite 1

CSR AI Spam Fix

Calls purchased from a marketplace cannot be billed as spam. Current CSR AI billing (all calls charged, including spam) would undermine the economics of paid call buying.

Prerequisite 2

SMS Follow-up on Dropped Calls

Many calls end without a live booking. AI-driven SMS follow-up captures the long-tail appointments from calls where the homeowner didn't convert on the first attempt.

04 Personas

Primary

The Owner-Operator in the Field

  • Solo or 1–5 employee shop (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, cleaning, pest)
  • No dedicated office staff or CSR
  • Phone is his/her direct line — rings while on a job
  • Currently spending on Angi, Thumbtack, or Google LSA with mixed results
  • Can't reliably answer inbound calls during the day; after hours is completely unmanaged
  • Motivated by: booked jobs per dollar, not lead volume
  • Pain: "I bought a lead and by the time I called back the homeowner had already booked someone"
Secondary

Small Crew Owner with Part-Time Staff

  • 5–20 employees, seasonal business (HVAC, roofing, landscaping)
  • Has someone answering phones part-time but not 24/7
  • Currently trying CSR AI or HCP Assist to cover the gaps
  • CSR AI spam charge problem lands hardest here — paying for bad calls on minute-based plans
  • Motivated by: off-hours call capture and appointment conversion rates
Key Insight

The Pro who most needs call buying is the same Pro who most needs AI answering. They are the same persona. This is why the solution pairing (call buying + CSR AI) is natural — it's not two separate products for two separate segments.

The Differentiated Position

No competitor currently pairs call buying + AI answering + SMS follow-up in one product. This combination is unclaimed territory.

Key Observations

  • ServiceTitan is the only major FSM investing in AI Voice at scale — Contact Center Pro is a full AI agent, not just a spam blocker. This is the competitive threat.
  • eLocal has the call volume (13M leads/year) and the exclusive call product HCP needs. They are already an integrated partner — scope of that integration is the open question.
  • Pros who want calls without a contract are already going outside HCP to ResultCalls: "they are so easy to work with, credit all our disputed calls, and don't require a balance."

The Prerequisites in Detail

Prerequisite 1 — CSR AI Spam Fix

Call buying only makes financial sense if the AI answering product is trustworthy. Right now, 3.81% of CSR AI calls are spam, and every one is billed. Pros on minute-based plans are churning (first-cycle bills as high as $1,095). If we sell a Pro on buying calls from eLocal and those calls route into CSR AI alongside existing spam, the spam charge problem amplifies. Engineering has LLM-based spam classification already (~6% of calls flagged post-call); billing exemption for those calls is the missing piece. The real-time in-call version (agent listening to transcript, ends call early) doesn't exist yet.

Prerequisite 2 — SMS Follow-up on Dropped/Unanswered Calls

Not every purchased call will book live. Some homeowners hang up before the AI closes, some are in the middle of a decision, some have bad connections. An AI-driven SMS follow-up sequence — triggered when a call ends without a booking — captures the long tail. The internal roadmap (3-month agentic plan, Feature #6) targets 20% conversion on follow-ups. Critical requirement: it must work for new prospects, not just existing customers. The current missed-call SMS only fires for saved contacts, which means it misses the most valuable segment: net-new homeowners calling for the first time.

08 Business Value & Revenue Model

The "Marketing Employee" Framing

At $49–59/month, Marketing AI + Call Marketplace is not a software expense — it's adding a dedicated marketing employee to the Pro's team. A single incremental booked job at ~$200–300 average revenue pays for 4–6 months of the product. The ROI test: "Did you get at least one more job this month?" For an owner-operator who was previously missing after-hours calls and losing paid leads to competitors, the answer is almost always yes.

Why "Warm Cohort" Changes the Business Case

Framing this as "3% of 55,000 Pros" is the wrong lens. The right lens: 7,000 Pros are already buying leads inside HCP. This is a conversion/upgrade motion — replace their current spend on shared data leads with exclusive inbound calls. A 25% conversion of warm lead buyers = 1,750 Pros on day one of scale. The remaining 48,000 Pros are a second-order expansion, not the launch target.
